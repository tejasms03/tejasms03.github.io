About Me

My name is Tejas Sriganesh

I am a Mechatronics and Robotics masters student @ NYU with a strong focus on intelligent robotic systems, automation, and software-driven control. I work across both software and hardware layers, developing solutions that integrate perception, motion planning, control algorithms, and real-world robotic platforms. My experience spans both research-driven development and practical engineering applications.

I have worked with technologies and tools such as Python, C++, and ROS/ROS2, along with reinforcement learning and computer vision systems. I regularly work in Linux-based environments using Git, and develop on hardware platforms including Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and embedded motor control systems.

I also use simulation environments and tools like Fusion 360 to design, test, and validate robotic systems.

My interest in robotics and software engineering has developed through academic research, hands-on projects, and industry experience. I am deeply committed to continuous learning and long-term technical growth, always pushing myself forward both professionally and personally.
