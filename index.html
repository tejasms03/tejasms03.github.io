About Me

My name is Tejas Sriganesh, and I am a Robotics and Software Engineer with a strong background in robotics, automation, and intelligent systems. I have experience working across both software and hardware, with a focus on robot motion planning, reinforcement learning, computer vision, and embedded systems. I have worked with technologies and tools such as Python, ROS/ROS2, C++, OpenCV, Reinforcement Learning frameworks, Linux, Git, Arduino, Fusion 360, and simulation environments, applying them to real-world robotic systems including quadrupeds, humanoid mechanisms, robotic arms, and multi-robot systems.

Me and my Work

  • What tech am I familiar with?

    I currently work with technologies such as Python, C++, ROS/ROS2, Linux, and Git for robotics and software development. My experience also includes reinforcement learning frameworks, computer vision tools like OpenCV, simulation environments such as PyBullet, and embedded platforms including Arduino and Raspberry Pi, along with CAD tools like Fusion 360 for system design and prototyping.

  • My Experience

    I have experience working in research-driven engineering environments, including my role as a Robotics Engineering Intern at the Technical University of Munich (TUM). My work involved developing intelligent robotic systems, simulation and modeling, reinforcement learning, and real-world system integration, combining academic research with hands-on engineering projects.

  • My Education

    I am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Mechatronics, Robotics, and Automation Engineering at New York University (NYU), Tandon School of Engineering. I previously earned a Bachelor of Engineering in Mechanical Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS Pilani). In addition to my academic training, I completed a Research Internship at the Technical University of Munich (TUM) at the Munich Institute of Robotics and Machine Intelligence, where I worked on mobile robotics, reinforcement learning, and robotic locomotion. This background has provided me with a strong foundation in robotics, automation, and software-driven systems.

  • What are my main research interests?

    My research interests include mobile robotics, robotic locomotion, motion planning, reinforcement learning, multi-robot systems, and sim-to-real transfer. I am particularly interested in developing intelligent control and learning-based methods that enable robots to operate reliably in complex real-world environments.

  • What areas of robotics do I specialize in?

    I specialize in mobile robotics, robotic manipulators, and multi-robot systems, with a strong focus on control, motion planning, and learning-based approaches. My work spans simulation, hardware integration, and real-world testing of robotic platforms.

  • What types of robotics projects have I worked on?

    I have worked on a wide range of robotics projects, including quadruped and hexapod robots, robotic arms with inverse kinematics control, gesture-controlled humanoid systems, and multi-robot task allocation and path planning. These projects involved design, simulation, control, and real-world deployment.

  • How do I approach problem-solving?

    I approach problem-solving by first building a strong theoretical understanding of the system, followed by iterative experimentation through simulation and prototyping. I prioritize modular design, data-driven decision making, and continuous testing to identify issues early and improve system performance efficiently.

  • How do I work in a team?

    I have experience working in multidisciplinary teams through my involvement with CRISS (Consortium for Research in Space Systems) and various extracurricular activities. As Mechanical Design Lead at CRISS, I collaborated closely with engineers across mechanical, electrical, and software domains, contributing to system integration and problem-solving. Additionally, my leadership roles in the college comedy club strengthened my communication, coordination, and teamwork skills. These experiences have enabled me to work effectively in collaborative environments, manage responsibilities, and contribute toward shared objectives.

  • What leadership experience do I have?

    I have held leadership roles as Mechanical Design Lead at CRISS, where I coordinated design and manufacturing efforts for a robotic arm, and as President of a college comedy club. These roles helped me develop skills in technical leadership, team coordination, mentorship, and effective communication.

  • What research and publication experience do I have?

    I have published research in the field of robotics and multi-agent systems, including work on drone swarms and collaborative target tracking in IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology (TVT). My research experience includes problem formulation, algorithm development, simulation and experimental validation, and technical writing for peer-reviewed journals.
